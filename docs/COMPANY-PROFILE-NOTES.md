## ⚠️ Read this first: the profile contains almost no text

The supplied "company profile" is **4 pages, 16 embedded images, and ZERO text fonts**. It was
produced with "Microsoft: Print To PDF" and every page is a picture. There is no extractable
copy — no history paragraph, no services list, no values statement, no warranty terms, no team
bios, no accreditation detail.

I extracted all 8 substantive images and read them visually. What follows is derived from
**photographic evidence only**.

**Consequence for the build:** the brief asked me to use the company profile to establish
history, exact services, positioning, tone of voice, customer promises, warranty terms and
business personality. **The document does not contain any of that.** Those items remain
outstanding and are listed in `FACT-VERIFICATION-REGISTER.md`.

## 1. The logo is INDIGO, not gold

**Extracted:** `client-assets/logos/vision-motors-logo-indigo-RASTER-606x251.jpg`
Sampled dominant colour: **`#290F74`** — a deep indigo/violet. Italic serif "V" + "ision",
with "Motors" set below in a heavier serif.

This **contradicts the brand direction in the approved demo**, which uses brass `#C9A24B` on
black. That brass was sampled from gold embroidery on the team's black polo shirts — which is
real (confirmed again in two of the three team portraits) — but it is a *uniform treatment*,
not the primary brand mark.

Both uniforms exist in the supplied photos:
- **Khaki/stone shirt** with the indigo logo + an embroidered first name
- **Black polo** with gold/brass logo (×2)

**This is a decision for the client, not for me to make silently.** See the Phase 1 report
§ Brand direction for the three options and my recommendation.

⚠️ **Still no vector logo.** The supplied file is a 606×251 raster JPEG embedded in a PDF. It is
too small for a website header at 2× and has JPEG artefacts on the edges. **Vector originals
(SVG/AI/EPS) remain outstanding** — this was on the Phase 1 asset request and was not delivered.

## 2. The address conflict is RESOLVED

**Extracted:** `client-assets/premises/premises-1059-steve-biko-frontage-541x374.jpg`

Two large signboards on the main building both carry the identical address block, which I read
at 12× magnification:

```
012 335 0070
867 Voortrekkersweg
1059 Steve Biko Road
Wonderboom South
```

**Vision Motors' own signage prints 867 Voortrekkersweg and 1059 Steve Biko Road together as one
address.** Steve Biko Road was renamed from Voortrekker Road in 2012, and the premises was
evidently renumbered at the same time; the sign carries both so long-standing customers can
still find it.

This resolves the conflict that was blocking the demo. The directory listings showing
`867 Voortrekkers Rd` are **not a different business or a stale second site** — they are the
same premises under its pre-2012 address.

→ **Canonical trading address: 1059 Steve Biko Road, Wonderboom South, Pretoria.**
→ `867 Voortrekkersweg` should be treated as a historical alias: useful for NAP clean-up on
directories, never published as a current address.

The kerb outside is painted `VISION MOTORS` and `1059`, which corroborates it independently.

## 3. There is a SECOND premises — "The Engine Shop"

**Extracted:** `client-assets/premises/premises-engine-shop-frontage-560x156.jpg`

A visually distinct building — different structure, different paintwork, different street
aspect — carrying:

- **"ENGINE SHOP"** in large red lettering on the fascia
- The **Vision Motors** logo alongside it
- A wall sign reading **"RANGER & BT50"**

This is a dedicated engine facility, separate from the car service and repair centre. It was
not mentioned anywhere in the earlier research, does not appear on the current live website,
and is not in any directory listing I have seen.

**This is the single most commercially significant find in the supplied material**, for two
reasons:

1. It converts "engine reconditioning" from a claimed service into a **visibly separate,
   purpose-built business unit** — proof, not a bullet point.
2. **"RANGER & BT50" is a specific, defensible, high-value specialism.** The Ford Ranger and
   Mazda BT-50 share a platform and engine family, and are among the highest-volume bakkies in
   South Africa. A workshop advertising that pairing on its wall is claiming real depth in one
   engine family — which is exactly the kind of narrow, provable expertise that ranks and
   converts, and which the demo's generic "engine reconditioning" positioning was leaving on
   the table.

⚠️ **Inference requiring confirmation:** the photo shows no street number. It is *likely* that
this is **1197 Steve Biko Road** — the second onboarding address — but that is my inference from
"two premises, two addresses", not something I can see. **Blocking item.**

Also visible on the fascia: a blue banner and additional partner logos, too low-resolution to
read.

## 6. What the profile did NOT provide

Everything below was requested and is **absent**:

- Company history / founding year / origin story
- Written services list
- Written positioning or values
- Tone-of-voice material or any prose at all
- **Warranty terms** — duration, mileage, coverage, exclusions, parts vs labour
- **RMI/MIWA membership evidence** — see below
- Team roles, titles, qualifications, years of service
- Operating processes
- Workshop, engine, gearbox, diagnostic or completed-work photography

### The RMI/MIWA file contains logos only

`1784731964038-RMI_en_Miwa_logos.docx` is a Word file containing **two images and no text**:
- `rmi-logo-270x148.png` — RMI oval, navy `#202964` + red `#E30713`
- `miwa-logo-270x148.png` — MIWA wordmark, red `#C9252B`

**Possessing a logo file is not evidence of membership.** There is no membership certificate,
no membership number, no grading document, no expiry date and no letter of good standing.

Per the brief's own instruction — *"only display RMI membership, MIWA membership, grading,
accreditation when directly supported by the supplied documents"* — **these badges cannot go on
the site yet.** What is needed is a membership certificate or the membership number, which can
then be checked against the RMI register.

Both logos are also only 270×148 and are almost certainly subject to brand-usage rules
(clear-space, minimum size, no recolouring). Request the official usage guidelines with the
certificate.
