## 3. The Customer

**Primary profiles**

1. **Out-of-motor-plan owner** (volume) — vehicle 3+ years old, facing dealer pricing for the
   first time, looking for a trustworthy independent. This is the segment Bosch Car Service
   Pretoria and Car Service City both target explicitly.
2. **Major-failure owner** (value) — engine or gearbox has failed; facing a R40k–R120k decision;
   has a dealer quote and is looking for a competent alternative.
3. **DSG / mechatronic owner** (most defensible) — dual-clutch fault, limp mode. Very few
   Pretoria workshops can do this work.
4. **Breakdown** (urgent entry point) — car won't move; converts directly into a repair job.

**Trigger moment:** something has gone wrong, or a warning light is on. Rarely planned.

**Arrival state:** distress + trust-critical. Consequence: speed and reassurance first,
click-to-call co-equal with the form, plain language over jargon.

**Top 5 objections, ranked**

| # | Objection (customer words) | Source | Answer strategy | Where on page |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "They'll do work I never approved and I'll get a shock bill" | MIWA red flag #1; competitors dedicate homepage lines to it | Written approval policy — nothing beyond the quote without your say-so | Trust strip · Why-us · Process · FAQ |
| 2 | "The final bill won't match the quote" | MIWA red flag #3 | Diagnose → quote → you decide, made explicit as a step | Process · FAQ |
| 3 | "Will they honour the warranty if it fails again?" | **Client's own 1-star Google review** (Marko, Jan 2022 — R40k reconditioned engine, warranty repair refused, charged R35k) | Publish real warranty terms once confirmed | ⚠️ **Currently unanswerable — terms unverified** |
| 4 | "Servicing outside the dealer will void my warranty" | Competition Commission clause 5.3 documents this exact knowledge gap | Right-to-choose section with accurate caveats | Customer rights section · FAQ |
| 5 | "How long will my car be there?" | **Client's own 1-star review** cites a 6-week wait | Honest "we'll tell you once we've seen it" — no invented number | Process · FAQ |

Also live: "they'll fit cheap parts" (#6), "I won't understand what they tell me" (#7),
"can they even work on my car?" (#8).

**The meta-objection:** #1, #2, #6 and #7 are one problem — **asymmetric information**. Every
high-converting element on competitor sites is an information-symmetry device. The homepage's
central idea is *"you'll see exactly what's wrong before you spend a cent."*

**Proof preferences (ranked for this niche):** real named reviews → credentials (RMI/MIWA) →
years trading → human faces/names → process transparency → workshop photography.

**Emotional arc:** arrives *anxious and expecting to be taken advantage of* → must leave
*confident these people will find the real fault and tell me the truth about it*.
