## 0. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — the one thing that changes everything

Vision Motors' current homepage sells a **generic mechanic**. Its own `/services` page sells a
**specialist driveline and engine-rebuild workshop**. The second business is far more valuable,
far more defensible, and almost invisible online.

The homepage currently promotes: engine repair, gearbox repair, belt change, brake repair, oil
change, fuel cleaning. The `/services` page — which almost nobody lands on — promotes: **engine
reconditioning and rebuilds, automatic and manual gearbox overhauls, transfer cases, propshafts,
differentials, DSG dual-clutch gearboxes, mechatronic units, and Ford specialism.**

That second list is work most independents cannot do and most dealers charge enormously for. It
is the actual business. The homepage buries it.

**The plan below rebuilds the homepage around the real business.**

> **The central creative idea for this homepage: "You'll see exactly what's wrong before you

> what the business already does, and it is directly supported by the Nissan testimonial and the

**Objections 3 and 5 are the client's own reputational scar tissue.** Eight one-star reviews
(11% of total) exist. The honest response is not to hide them but to make warranty terms and
turnaround explicit on the page. This is a genuine business conversation to have with the client,
not just a copy decision.

## 5. PRIMARY CONVERSION ACTION

### Recommended primary CTA: **"Book Your Car In"**

Used identically in the header, hero, after services, after proof, and in the final CTA.
All instances scroll to / open the same booking form.

**Why this wording rather than the alternatives in the brief:**

- **"Book a Vehicle Inspection"** — reject. In South African usage "vehicle inspection" strongly
  implies a *pre-purchase inspection* or *roadworthy test*. It would attract the wrong enquiries
  and confuse the actual customer.
- **"Request a Service Booking"** — reject. Excludes the repair and breakdown customers, who are
  the higher-value segments.
- **"Book Your Car In"** — recommend. It is how the customer already speaks, covers service,
  repair and diagnosis equally, is low-commitment, and translates cleanly to Afrikaans
  ("Bespreek jou kar in").

**Secondary conversion:** `Call 012 335 0070` — click-to-call everywhere, persistent in the
sticky mobile bar. For a workshop, calls will likely outnumber form fills; the build treats the
call as a first-class tracked conversion, not an afterthought.

**Tertiary (conditional):** WhatsApp. **Not built until confirmed.** A mobile number
(+27 61 642 5591) appears on MechanicBuddy but is not on the client's own site. The component
will be built and left disabled behind a config flag.

**Form fields** (short by design — every field costs conversions):
Name · Phone · Vehicle make & model · What's wrong / what you need · (optional) Email.
Preferred-contact and detailed-description fields are deliberately omitted from v1.

**★ Change 1 — a symptom-led band (§4) ahead of the services grid.**
The visitor does not arrive thinking "I need a gearbox overhaul." They arrive thinking "it's
making a noise." Every competitor leads with a service taxonomy, which is the business's mental
model, not the customer's. Leading with symptoms matches the actual arrival state, and it feeds
the highest-intent long-tail search cluster.

**★ Change 2 — a warranty / right-to-choose section, replacing a generic "trust" block.**
This is the strategic finding of the research. Every competitor in the Wonderboom / Pretoria
North cluster sells *accreditation* — identical RMI badges, an identical 6-month/10,000km
guarantee. **Nobody sells the customer's rights.** The Competition Commission itself documented
(clause 5.3) that consumers don't know them. This is uncontested ground with primary-source
backing, and it captures the customer at the exact moment they decide to leave their dealer.

**Sections from the brief I recommend cutting from v1:** a separate "problem/agitation" block
(the symptom band does this job better and faster) and a standalone towing section (towing
becomes an entry in the symptom band plus a footer/FAQ item — it is an entry point, not a
homepage pillar).

## 12. VISUAL DIRECTION

### The brand is gold on black — and the current site doesn't know it

The real brand mark is visible on the team's uniforms in the one authentic photo on the site:
an embroidered **gold/brass "Vision Motors"** wordmark on **black** polos. The logo file is a
white-on-dark wordmark.

Meanwhile the current website uses **four unrelated CTA colours**: royal blue `#2E56CC`,
indigo `#302B71`, slate `#394B56` and red `#E3002B`. There is no accent discipline, so no colour
means "next step".

### Proposed direction — "precision workshop, not race track"

Deep near-black surfaces with a **brass/gold accent reserved exclusively for the primary CTA**,
per OS design-token rules. This reads as *established, technical, premium* — and deliberately
avoids the red/black racing cliché the brief rules out.

Draft tokens (OS structure, brand-derived values — to be confirmed against the real logo files):

```ts
// Values below are PROVISIONAL — to be sampled from the vector logo.
primary:   "#C9A24B"  primaryDark: "#A8842F"  primaryLight: "#DCBE79"  // brass
accent:    "#C9A24B"  accentDark:  "#A8842F"  // reserved for the primary CTA ONLY
ink:       "#0B0B0B"  charcoal:    "#141414"
graphite:  "#1C1C1C"  steel:       "#2A2A2A"
mist:      "#F5F5F4"  bone:        "#E7E5E4"
```

Open question for the design step: with brass serving as both brand and conversion colour, the
CTA loses some of its "this colour = next step" distinctiveness. The alternative is a restrained
technical blue as `primary` (links, icons) with brass held exclusively for CTAs. I lean toward
the second and will confirm once I have the real logo.

Exact hex values will be sampled from the vector logo once the client supplies it —
`SYSTEM/01-DESIGN-TOKENS.md` §6 requires contrast pairs verified at token time, not QA time.

**Type:** Inter (body) + Space Grotesk (display) — technical, industrial, excellent at small
sizes, already proven in the OS. Two families maximum, self-hosted via `next/font`.

**Motion:** OS defaults only — `[0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]` easing, 0.7s section reveals, 0.08s stagger,
`viewport={{ once: true }}`, full `prefers-reduced-motion` support. No count-up animations except
on verified numbers (i.e. "1992" / "34 years" only).

## 14. COMPONENT AND CONFIG ARCHITECTURE

**Important context:** Website OS 2.0 is at Phase 1. `SYSTEM/`, `PIPELINE/` and `RESEARCH/` exist;
**the ENGINE (shared component library) has not been built.** Per `SYSTEM/00-OPERATING-MODEL.md`,
until ENGINE exists we **harvest patterns from `SHOWCASE/`** rather than inventing new ones.

I audited all five production sites. This build harvests **signage** (closest analogue: local
Pretoria trade services, richest config surface, `types/site.ts`) crossed with **multi-risk**
(best lead-capture machinery, server-route webhook, analytics).

### Stack — pinned, per `SYSTEM/02-TECH-STACK.md`

Next.js 14.2 · React 18.3 · TypeScript 5.4 strict · Tailwind 3.4 · Framer Motion 11 ·
lucide-react. **Five runtime dependencies. No clsx, no zod, no form library, no UI kit.**

### Non-negotiable conventions harvested from SHOWCASE

- **The config rule — absolute.** No copy, phone number, address, stat or URL hardcoded in any
  component. 100% compliance across all five production sites; it stays 100%.
- **Three phone fields** in `site-config`: `phone` (E.164 for schema), `phoneDisplay` (human),
  `phoneLink` (`tel:`). Every CTA reads these, never a literal.
- **`icon` is a string key**, never a component — keeps config a pure data layer.
- **`SectionHeading` API:** `{ eyebrow, title: ReactNode, description, align, tone, as }` —
  the 3-of-5-sites majority API plus bougasvlei's `as` prop.
- **Booking form → `/api/booking` server route → `GHL_WEBHOOK_URL` → `/thank-you`.**
  Server-only env var; the webhook URL never reaches the browser bundle. Flat snake_case payload
  keys for easy GHL field mapping. Graceful degradation when the env var is unset so preview
  builds don't silently lose leads. **No webhook secrets in the frontend.**
- **`reviews-config.ts` ships empty with a comment** until the client confirms review consent —
  the anti-fabrication pattern already used on multi-risk and bougasvlei.
- **z-index contract:** sticky bar `z-50`, floating FAB `z-40`, header `z-50`.
- **Schema in `lib/schema.ts`** from day one (not inline in layout) — there will be
  AutoRepair + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList.

### Reusability for the full site later

Every section is config-driven, so the future `/services`, `/engine-reconditioning`,
`/gearbox-repairs` and location pages reuse the same components with different config. `routes.ts`
is the single source for nav **and** sitemap. This is what makes the homepage a foundation rather
than a one-off.
